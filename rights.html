Know Your Rights

Reproductive & Voting Rights

Language is power. Understanding your rights is the first step toward protecting them.

Reproductive Rights

Reproductive rights are fundamental human rights related to reproduction and reproductive health. They include the right to make decisions about your own body without coercion or violence.

  • Access to accurate information about reproductive health
  • Access to contraception and family planning services
  • The right to decide if, when, and how many children to have
  • Access to safe and legal abortion services
  • Protection from forced sterilization
  • Access to quality maternal healthcare
  • Freedom from discrimination based on reproductive choices

Voting Rights

Voting is a fundamental right in a democracy. Every eligible citizen has the right to have their voice heard through free and fair elections.

  • Right to register to vote without discrimination
  • Right to vote in federal, state, and local elections
  • Right to accessible polling locations
  • Protection from voter intimidation and suppression
  • Right to request time off work to vote
  • Right to language assistance at polling places
  • Right to a provisional ballot if issues arise

Know Your State Laws

Reproductive rights vary significantly by state. Stay informed about laws in your area.

Reproductive Rights by State

Laws regarding abortion access, contraception, and reproductive healthcare differ across the country. Some states have strong protections while others have severe restrictions.

Check your state's current laws to understand what rights and services are available to you.

Voting Laws by State

Each state has different rules for voter registration, ID requirements, early voting, and mail-in ballots. Know your state's specific requirements before Election Day.

Verify your registration and find your polling location well in advance.
